Vintage Classic Cars: Restoration tips

Restore. Refine. Relive.



Ultimate Guide to Classic Wheel & Tire Revival


Wheels and tires are more than just moving parts—they shape the soul, stance, and spirit of every vintage car. Whether you're restoring a forgotten roadster from a dusty barn or maintaining a prized garage-kept gem, proper wheel and tire rejuvenation ensures your vehicle looks authentic and drives safely. This detailed guide walks you through the best practices, techniques, and expert insight for restoring vintage wheels and tires with precision.



Inspection First: Know What You’re Dealing With

Before anything else, inspect the wheels and tires. Look for:

  • Rust or corrosion on metal wheels
  • Cracks or dryness in rubber
  • Bent rims or warping
  • Outdated tire treads

Tip: Take photos and note any manufacturing codes or markings—they might help identify original parts or manufacturing dates.


Cleaning: Start With a Fresh Surface

Use a degreaser or mild soap to remove decades of dirt, brake dust, and grime. For chrome, use non-abrasive cleaners. For steel wheels

  • Use a wire brush to remove surface rust
  • Rinse thoroughly and dry completely

Pro Tip: Avoid acidic cleaners—they can eat into vintage materials.


Rust Treatment & Paint Restoration

If your wheels are metal:

  • Apply rust converter or rust remover
  • Sand smooth once dry
  • Prime and repaint using wheel-safe enamel or powder coating

    • Match the color with the car’s original look, or go for a custom period-correct finish.


Tire Check or Replacement

Vintage tires may look cool but are often unsafe.

  • Check for dry rot, bulges, or uneven wear
  • If replacing, search for reproduction tires that match the original specs but use modern rubber compounds

    • You can find vintage-style tires from brands like Coker Tire or BFGoodrich Classic Series.


Balancing and Alignment

After restoration or new installation:

  • Balance the wheels to avoid shaking or uneven driving
  • Align them to prevent unnecessary tire wear

    • Even a small misalignment can ruin a fresh restoration over time.


Don’t Forget Hubcaps & Lug Nuts

Often overlooked, these details finish the look:

  • Polish or repaint original hubcaps
  • Replace rusty or mismatched lug nuts with correct-period pieces

    • It’s the little details that make your restoration look professional.


Document Your Process

Take before and after shots. Share your progress on forums or social media groups. It helps others and records your hard work.
